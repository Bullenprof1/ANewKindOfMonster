Upcoming Japanese streaming television series Godzilla Singular Point Promotional release poster ゴジラ S.P ＜シンギュラポイント＞ (Gojira S.P <Shingyura Pointo>) Anime television series Directed by Atsushi Takahashi Written by Toh EnJoe Music by Kan Sawada Studio Bones Orange Licensed by Netflix (worldwide) Original network Tokyo MX, KBS, BS11, SUN Original run April 1, 2021 – present Episodes 3 (List of episodes)  Anime and manga portal Godzilla Singular Point (Japanese: ゴジラ S.P ＜シンギュラポイント＞, Hepburn: Gojira Shingyura Pointo) is a Japanese anime television series co-produced by the animation studios Bones and Orange. Licensed by Netflix and directed by Atsushi Takahashi, the series premiered on March 25, 2021, on Netflix in Japan, and on Tokyo MX and other channels on April 1, 2021. Nakagawa (マキタ・K・中川, Makita Kei Nakagawa) Voiced by: Hiromichi Tezuka[2] Tilda Mira (ティルダ・ミラー, Tiruda Mirā) Voiced by: Masako Isobe[2] Michael Steven (マイケル・スティーブン, Maikeru Sutībun) Voiced by: Kenta Miyake[2] Production[edit] On October 6, 2020, Toho Animation and Netflix announced plans for an anime Godzilla series, titled Godzilla Singular Point, it premiered in April 1, 2021 on Tokyo MX and other channels. Production for the show is by Bones in partnership with Orange, blending hand-drawn and computer animation styles respectively. Atsushi Takahashi serves as the director with scripts by Toh EnJoe, and music composed by Kan Sawada. Character design is handled by Blue Exorcist manga creator Kazue Kato, with former Studio Ghibli animator Eiji Yamamori designing the monsters.[3][4] The series' opening theme is performed by BiSH and titled "in case...", while Polkadot Stingray performed the ending theme song "Aoi" (青い, Blue).[5] The series debuted earlier on Netflix in Japan on March 25, 2021,[6] which will be followed by a global release sometime in June.[7] Episode list[edit] No. English localized title / English translated title Original Japanese title [8] Directed by Written by Original air date [9] 1 "Terzetto" / "A Distant Road Home" Transcription: "Haruka Naru Ieji" (Japanese: はるかなるいえじ) Noriyuki Nomata Takuma Suzuki Toh EnJoe April 1, 2021 (2021-04-01) 2 "Gamesome" / "Midsummer Monster Festival" Transcription: "Manatsu Oni Matsuri" (Japanese: まなつおにまつり) Daisuke Tsukushi Takuma Suzuki Toh EnJoe April 8, 2021 (2021-04-08) 3 "Tigerish" / "Nobae's Terror" Transcription: "Nobae no Kyōfu" (Japanese: のばえのきょうふ) Noriyuki Nomata Takuma Suzuki Toh EnJoe April 15, 2021 (2021-04-15) Marketing[edit] First images and poster art were released on October 26, 2020 during Netflix's Anime Festival, with the streaming service releasing a teaser trailer later that same day.[10][11] The teaser revealed that the series would air in April 2021 and would feature monsters from the Shōwa era of the Godzilla franchise, such as Rodan, Anguirus, Jet Jaguar, Titanosaurus, and Gabara.[12] On October 12, 2020, it was announced that Takahashi, EnJoe, Bones producer Naoki Amano, Orange producer Jiro Ando and Toho producer Takashi Yoshizawa would all appear in a panel during the Godzilla Fest Online 2020 event on November 3, 2020.[13] On February 12, 2021, the full design of Godzilla was revealed by Netflix.
