This article is about the Greek deity. For the moon of Jupiter, see Eupheme (moon). Eupheme (/juːˈfiːmiː/) was the ancient Greek female spirit of words of good omen, praise, acclaims, shouts of triumph, and applause. Her opposite was Momus and her sisters were Euthenia, Eucleia, and Philophrosyne. Along with her sisters, she was regarded as a member of the younger Charites.
