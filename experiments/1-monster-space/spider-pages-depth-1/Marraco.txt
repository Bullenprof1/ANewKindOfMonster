Marraco In Catalan mythology, a Marraco is a dragon that said have a wide mouth big enough to eat humans whole. (June 2020) (Learn how and when to remove this template message) For Lleida, the dragon is an animal totem of Lleida. We know of its existence in the fifth century. C. As a mythological animal never existed and its representation is free. For this animal was the old Ilergets' representation of their god, their symbol. The chiefs were regarded as children of God and as the most important leader was Indíbil the Marraco was considered his spiritual father. Events[edit] Today, there is an event that involves a monstrous figure of an animal-shaped dragon traveling with a vehicle at the Corpus Christi processions.
