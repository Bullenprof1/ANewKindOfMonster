Find sources: "API" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR (December 2009) (Learn how and when to remove this template message) Cebren was a Greek river-god,[1] whose river was located near Troy. He was the son of Oceanus and Tethys and he was the father of Asterope, Hesperia, who are sometimes considered to be the same person, and Oenone. The city Cebrene (also spelled Kebrene or Kevrin) was named for Cebren. References[edit] ^ Aaron J.
