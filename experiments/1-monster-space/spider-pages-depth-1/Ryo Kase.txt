Japanese actor Ryo Kase 加瀬 亮 Born (1974-11-09) 9 November 1974 (age 46) Yokohama, Japan Nationality Japanese Occupation Actor Years active 2000–present Agent Ryo Kase Office Known for Letters from Iwo Jima Hill of Freedom Outrage Like Someone in Love Restless Silence Height 1.74 m (5 ft 8 1⁄2 in) Website ryokaseoffice.com/index_english.html Ryo Kase (加瀬 亮, Kase Ryō, born November 9, 1974 in Yokohama) is a Japanese actor.[1][2] Contents 1 Early life 2 Career 2.1 International films 3 Filmography 3.1 Film 3.2 Television 4 Writings 5 Bibliography 6 Awards 6.1 2004 6.2 2007 6.3 2008 6.4 2010 6.5 2013 7 References 8 External links Early life[edit] Kase was born in Yokohama, Kanagawa prefecture. He moved to Bellevue, Washington in the United States soon after his birth, due to his father's job being transferred.[3] Career[edit] Kase made his screen debut in Sogo Ishii's Gojoe: Spirit War Chronicle in 2000.[3] He starred in Masayuki Suo's 2007 film I Just Didn't Do It.[4] International films[edit] He has also appeared in films such as Clint Eastwood's Letters from Iwo Jima, Michel Gondry's Tokyo!, Gus Van Sant's Restless,[5] Abbas Kiarostami's Like Someone in Love,[6][7] Hong Sang-soo's Hill of Freedom, Takeshi Kitano's Outrage,[8]/ Outrage Beyond,[9] Martin Scorsese's Silence and Paul Weitz's Bel Canto.
