Old Kyoto Central Telephone Office Osaka Central Post Office (right) Tetsuro Yoshida (吉田 鉄郎, Yoshida Tetsurō, May 18, 1894 - September 8, 1956) was a Japanese architect. He graduated from Tokyo University and entered the Ministry of Communications in 1919. He designed many Japanese post offices, telegraph offices, and related buildings in Japan.[1] He introduced Eastern architecture to the west, while incorporating Western architecture in his own designs, including architecture from Scandinavia, Germany, and the United States.[2] Major works[edit] Old Kyoto Central Telephone Office, 1926 Tokyo Central Post Office, 1931 Osaka Central Post Office, 1939 References[edit] ^ Steele, James (2017).
