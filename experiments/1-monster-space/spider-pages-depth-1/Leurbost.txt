It is approximately 6 miles (10 kilometres) south of Stornoway on the road to Harris. It is the main settlement in the area of the island known as North Lochs. Leurbost is within the parish of Lochs.[1] Population: Over 500 Languages : English (UK) Gàidhlig (Gaelic) Football Team: Lochs FC Football Pitch/Best pies served at: Creagan Dubh Pies served by: Pie Ladies Contents 1 History 2 Economy 3 References 4 External links History[edit] In 1856 reports circulated of a 'sea-serpent' in a fresh water loch near the town.[2] The creature, referred to as Searrach Uisge, was said to be eel-like, 40 feet (12 metres) long and raised comparisons to the mythical Kelpie.[2][3] Economy[edit] Amenities in the village include a community centre; a school, Sgoil nan Loch, (which replaced the old Leurbost as well as Fidigarry (Ranish), Knockiandue (Keose) and Balallan schools); a petrol station and a shop.
