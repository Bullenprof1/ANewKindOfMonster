Eupraxia, in ancient Greek religion, was the personification of well-being. She is mentioned once by Aeschylus, who cites a proverb according to which Eupraxia is the daughter of another two personifications, Peitharchia and Soter.[1] References[edit] ^ Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes, 223 ff.
