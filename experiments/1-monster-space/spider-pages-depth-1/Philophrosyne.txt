This article is about the Greek deity. For the moon of Jupiter, see Philophrosyne (moon). Philophrosyne /fɪləˈfrɒzɪniː/ was the ancient Greek female spirit of welcome, friendliness, and kindness. Her sisters were Euthenia, Eupheme, and Eucleia. Along with her sisters, she was regarded as a member of the younger Charites. According to the Orphic fragments, Philophrosyne was the daughter of Hephaestus and Aglaia. References[edit] Geffcken, Johannes, The Last Days of Greco-Roman Paganism, North Holland Pub. Co., 1978.
