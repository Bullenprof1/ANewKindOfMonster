Edwards Douglas Esper Edited by Drew Fortier Release date February 2021 (2021-02) (Mad Monster Party) October 12, 2021 (2021-10-12) Country United States Language English Dwellers is an American found footage horror film written and directed by Drew Fortier.[1] It is the first film greenlit by Ellefson Films, formed by Megadeth bassist David Ellefson.[2][3] The plot of the film follows a documentary film crew who eventually go missing while uncovering the truths behind the disappearances within a homeless community.[4][5] The film stars Drew Fortier, James L. Edwards, Douglas Esper as well as David Ellefson portraying himself as a featured cameo.[6][7] Contents 1 Cast 2 Production 3 Premiere 4 Awards 5 Release 6 References 7 External links Cast[edit] Drew Fortier as Drew James L. Edwards as James Douglas Esper as Doug Omar Baig as Dotani Rick Jermain as Detective Jenkins Dustin Boltjes Mitch Lafon as Mitch Lafon Jeffrey Hatrix as Serious Hobo Tharasa DiMeo as Sewer Hobo David Ellefson as Ellefson Isabelle Fox as Mr. Hands Production[edit] Dwellers marks the first instance of thrash metal musician David Ellefson as a film producer with the formation of Ellefson Films; expanding his brand which also includes record label EMP Label Group and coffee company Ellefson Coffee Co.[8][9] Production began in May 2019 and as of August 2019, shooting is near completion.[10] Included locations are: Barberton and Cleveland, OH., Chicago, IL., Indianapolis, IN., and Nashville, TN.[11][12] In August 2019, Ellefson Films released the poster and teaser trailer for the film which includes a voiceover by Mitch Lafon from Rock Talk with Mitch Lafon as well as the Nine Inch Nails song The Day the World Went Away covered by Indie rock band The Foxery.[11] In November 2019, Ellefson Films released the full trailer for the film which features a cover of the Nine Inch Nails track We're in This Together performed by Ajna Cova .[13][14] According to a Q&A panel Ellefson Films had held at Rue Morgue's Frightmare in the Falls convention, David Ellefson and Drew Fortier explained that the film will be a gritty Cinema verite experience with the story being told through the documentary being filmed as well as the behind the scene footage that the Drew character insists on being shot. Dwellers is described by Ellefson and Fortier as being heavily influenced by the films The Blair Witch Project and C.H.U.D..[15] Premiere[edit] In February 2021, Dwellers had its world premiere at the Mad Monster Party horror convention in Concord, North Carolina. The film's producer David Ellefson as well as director Drew Fortier were in attendance for the event where they were presented with the Best Horror Feature award from the event's film festival.[16] Awards[edit] As of March 2021, the film has been officially selected into over a dozen film festivals and has garnered the Best Feature award from the Screamwriting Film Festival, Best Horror Feature award from the Mad Monster Film Festival, Most Disturbing Scenes award from Horror Movie Bowl Festival, Best Horror Feature from the Vegas Movie Awards, Best Producer Award, given to David Ellefson, from the Vegas Movie Awards, and Best Director award given to Drew Fortier from The Thing in the Basement Horror Fest.[17][18][19] Release[edit] In March 2021, it was announced that Dwellers would be released on Blu Ray, DVD, and Digital on October 12, 2021 via Ellefson Films (Amped/Alliance).[20][21] References[edit] ^ Blabbermouth (2019-05-31).
