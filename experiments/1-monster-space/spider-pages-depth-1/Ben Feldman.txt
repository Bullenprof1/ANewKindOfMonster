Contents 1 Early life and education 2 Career 2.1 Other pursuits 3 Personal life 4 Filmography 4.1 Film 4.2 Television 5 References 6 External links Early life and education[edit] Feldman was born in Potomac, Maryland. His mother is Kris Feldman,[1] and his father, Robert "Bob" Feldman, runs a Maryland advertising agency.[2][3] Feldman is from a Jewish family and attended Conservative Jewish and Orthodox Jewish synagogues and schools.[4][5] He has a sister, Morgan.[1] His maternal aunt, Susan Feniger, is a chef in Los Angeles.[6] He was involved in acting camp and school theater from the age of 6. He attended Winston Churchill High School in Potomac. He worked as a camp counselor in the theater and taught a videography class for several summers in Bethesda, and attended Ithaca College in New York, where he received a Bachelor of Fine Arts in acting.[7] After graduating, he moved to New York City, where he performed on Broadway and eventually moved to Los Angeles to act in film and television.[8] Career[edit] This section needs expansion. (January 2021) From 2009 to 2011, he was a regular on the television series Drop Dead Diva in the role of guardian angel Fred before leaving the series at the start of the fourth season.[9] In April 2012, he joined the cast of AMC's Mad Men as a regular, playing the character Michael Ginsberg.[10] More recently Feldman appeared as Ron LaFlamme on HBO's Silicon Valley[11] from 2014 to 2019, and from 2015 to 2021 played the lead Jonah Simms in the NBC sitcom Superstore.[12] Other pursuits[edit] Feldman is co-owner of a wine label, Angelica Cellars.[8] Personal life[edit] Feldman resides in Los Angeles, California.
