Find sources: "API" – news · newspapers · books · scholar · JSTOR (April 2013) (Learn how and when to remove this template message) Chinese horror is a term given[who?] to Chinese, Hong Kong, and Taiwanese films as part of the stream of Asian horror films. Like Korean and Japanese as well as other Asian horror films many focus on ghosts (yurei is also very common), supernatural environments, and suffering. Perhaps one of the best films for C-horror is The Eye directed by the Pang brothers which was later remade. There is also some comedy elements such as Bio Zombie, Troublesome Night film series, The Vampire Who Admires Me, and My Left Eye Sees Ghosts. Jiangshi[edit] Main article: Jiangshi fiction Jiangshi fiction, revolving around the hopping vampire or zombie, is a subgenre of Chinese horror.
